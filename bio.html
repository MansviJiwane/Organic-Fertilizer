Organic Fertilizer

Organic fertilizers promote healthy microbial activity, increasing soil fertility and supporting sustainable growth. Beneficial bacteria like Rhizobium and Azotobacter work to fix nitrogen and enhance plant health.

Inorganic Fertilizer

Inorganic fertilizers provide immediate nutrients to crops, but they disrupt soil health and reduce microbial diversity. Overuse leads to soil degradation and chemical residues in food.

Advantages of Organic Fertilizer

Organic fertilizers increase soil biodiversity by supporting microbes like Rhizobium, Azotobacter, and others. These microbes improve nitrogen fixation, reduce the need for chemical inputs, and help build long-term soil health.

Close

Disadvantages of Inorganic Fertilizer

Overusing inorganic fertilizers leads to soil acidification, depletion of beneficial microorganisms, and accumulation of toxic chemicals. This impacts plant health and can result in long-term environmental harm.
